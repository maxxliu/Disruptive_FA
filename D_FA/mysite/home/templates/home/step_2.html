Step 2: Forecasting Revenue Growth

 

Evaluate predictors of the future

Consider factors affecting revenue:

    Is the market/company growing, shrinking, stable?
    Is the market share of the company increasing or decreasing?
    Are there any new products driving sales?
    Are they about to lose the patent of their best product?

Take the following example

Company X has grown at 15% for the last 3 years, and your research says that demand for their products is not likely to slow down. Management says the company will continue to grow at 15%.

    While it could certainly continue to grow at 15% for the next 5 years, a competitor could enter and begin chipping away at market share
    Stay on the conservative side:
      Maybe predict another 2 years at 15%, then 2 years at 10% (new competitor enters in year 3), and a year at 8% (new competitor has experience and is starting to solidify its market share).
