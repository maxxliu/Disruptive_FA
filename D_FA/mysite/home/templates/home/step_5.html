Step 5: Finding Present Value of Future Cash Flows

 

How it's Calculated

Present Value = FCF1 / (1+r) + FCF2 / (1+r)2 + FCFn / (1+r)n + Terminal Value / (1+r)n

 

Discounting the future free cash flows for the N = 5- or 10-year period gives the present value of the future free cash flows (PV FCF)

r = discount rate (WACC in this case)
